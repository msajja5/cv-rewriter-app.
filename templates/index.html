Capturing Interview Audio (Zoom, Teams, Meet)

Web browsers cannot directly "hack" into Zoom or Teams to steal the audio if you are wearing headphones. You have two free options:

Option 1: The Easy Way (Use Speakers)
Do not wear headphones. Let the interviewer's voice come out of your laptop speakers. Your laptop microphone will naturally pick up their voice and transcribe it perfectly.
Option 2: The Pro Way (Wear Headphones)
If you must wear headphones, you need to route the meeting audio into your browser microphone using a free Virtual Audio Cable.
  1. Download and install VB-Cable (Windows/Mac) or BlackHole (Mac).
  2. In Zoom/Teams, set your Speaker output to "VB-Cable".
  3. In Chrome/Edge settings (or OS Sound settings), set your Microphone input to "VB-Cable".
  4. Note: You will also need to turn on "Listen to this device" in your OS sound settings so you can hear the interviewer in your headphones at the same time.

These keys are stored locally in your browser. If empty, the app uses built-in Vercel environment variables or mock fallbacks.
